### 3. Community Sentiment Signal
Today's Hacker News sentiment is defined by a **"Quality Control vs. Hype"** narrative. The most active discussions are not celebrating new capabilities, but rather auditing the fallout of the AI boom. The revelation that 90% of Claude-assisted code ends up in abandoned repos (Item #1) struck a nerve, validating the skeptical view that while AI increases code *volume*, it may not be increasing code *value*.

There is also a palpable sense of **strategic pivot fatigue** regarding OpenAI. The collapse of the Sora project and the Disney partnership is being discussed not as a failure of technology, but as a market correction—users are sensing that the "generate anything" era is ending, and the "make it reliable/profitable" era is beginning with mixed results. Finally, the "Tired of AI" thread, while lower in score, encapsulates a growing undercurrent of burnout among engineers who feel pressured to adopt agentic workflows that still require significant cleanup.
