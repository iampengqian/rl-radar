## 3. Trend Signal Analysis
Today's GitHub trending data reveals a decisive shift from foundational model training to **Agentic UX and Context Engineering**. The explosive growth of projects like `andrej-karpathy-skills` (+7.9k) and `claude-mem` (+1.9k) proves that the community's bottleneck has shifted from *how to train models* to *how to interact with them effectively*. Developers are realizing that raw model capabilities are insufficient without persistent memory and highly tuned system prompts. This marks the dawn of "Prompt-driven DevOps," where markdown files and memory-retrieval plugins are treated with the same rigor as traditional infrastructure.

Simultaneously, we are witnessing the **standardization of multi-agent frameworks**. The presence of OpenAI's official `openai-agents-python` alongside Vercel's `open-agents` indicates a transition from experimental agent swarms to production-ready, cloud-native agent architectures. 

Furthermore, **AI autonomous evolution** is emerging as a novel tech stack. Projects like `GenericAgent` and `evolver` introduce concepts like "genome evolution" and "skill trees" for AI. Instead of hard-coding agent behaviors, developers are building meta-systems that allow agents to dynamically adapt and optimize their own operational graphs. This aligns perfectly with recent industry pushes toward "Computer Use" agents, requiring systems that can learn from OS-level feedback loops in real-time.
